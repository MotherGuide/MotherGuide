# IMPLEMENETATION OF EMPRICAL INVESTIGATION IN MOTHERGUIDE

## Hypothesis 1: Nutrition Tip Relevance
Mothers who receive gestation-stage-specific nutrition tips twice weekly will show a 25% improvement in dietary compliance compared to those receiving general tips.

### Case Study 1: Nutrition Tip Effectiveness
### 1. Conception
Goal: Improve maternal nutrition through personalized guidance.

Hypothesis: Stage-specific tips increase compliance.

### 2. Design
Participants: 60 pregnant mothers split into two groups (specific vs. general tips).

#### Variables:

i. Independent: Tip specificity

ii. Dependent: Dietary compliance score

iii. Measurement: Weekly food logs and health worker assessments.

### 3. Execution
Group A receives tailored tips via app notifications.

Group B receives general pregnancy nutrition advice.

### 4. Analysis
Compare average compliance scores over agiven period of time say 4 weeks.

Use t-tests to assess statistical significance and differences between the 2 groups.

### 5. Interpretation
Group A shows 27% higher compliance.

Mothers report greater clarity and motivation.

### 6. Packaging
Document tip templates by gestation stage.

Recommend integration into all nutrition modules.

## Hypothesis 2: Emergency Response Speed
Mothers using the emergency alert button will receive assistance within 15 minutes in 80% of cases, compared to 40% for manual contact.

## Case Study 2: Emergency Alert Efficiency
### 1. Conception
Goal: Reduce response time for maternal emergencies.

Hypothesis: Automated alerts improve speed.

### 2. Design
Participants: 20 mothers flagged for high-risk pregnancies.

#### Variables:

i. Independent: Contact method (alert vs. manual)

ii. Dependent: Response time/period

iii. Measurement: App logs and health worker timestamps.

### 3. Execution
Track emergency interactions over 6 weeks.

Compare alert-triggered vs. manual calls.

### 4. Analysis
Calculate average response time per method.

Use Analysis of variance (ANOVA tests) to test differences.

### 5. Interpretation
Alert users received help in 12 minutes on average.

Manual users averaged 22 minutes.

85% of alerts met the 15-minute minimum waiting time.

### 6. Packaging
Recommend alert button as default for all users.

Train health workers on rapid response protocols.

## Hypothesis 3: Warning Sign Awareness
Mothers who view the “Warning Signs” section at least twice will correctly identify 3+ danger signs in 90% of cases.

## Case Study 3: Health Risk Recognition
### 1. Conception
Goal: Improve maternal awareness of danger signs.

### Hypothesis: Repeated exposure boosts recognition.

### 2. Design
Participants: 50 mothers across all trimesters.

#### Variables:

i. Independent: Exposure frequency

ii. Dependent: Quiz score

Measurement: In-app quiz with 5 danger signs.

### 3. Execution
Track section views via app analytics and logs.

Administer quiz after 2 weeks.

### 4. Analysis
Compare scores between low and high exposure groups.

Use chi-square test for categorical outcomes.

### 5. Interpretation
92% of high-exposure mothers scored 3+.

Only 58% of low-exposure mothers did.

### 6. Packaging
Add reminders to revisit warning signs weekly.

Include visuals and for better retention.

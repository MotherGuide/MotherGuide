Eat for Two — But Eat Smart, Not Double

During pregnancy, your body needs extra nutrients — but that doesn’t mean you should double your food portions. Instead of eating twice as much, focus on eating twice as healthy.

A balanced pregnancy diet should include:
Iron-rich foods – such as beans, spinach, liver, and fortified cereals to prevent anemia.
Calcium sources – like milk, yogurt, and small fish with bones to support your baby’s bone development.
Protein – from eggs, beans, groundnuts, fish, or lean meat to help your baby grow properly.
Fruits and vegetables – rich in vitamins and fiber to boost immunity and prevent constipation.
Plenty of clean water – to stay hydrated and support increased blood volume.
